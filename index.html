Content

I chose to design a wine commerical for a red wine brand that I made, Soule.

Theme

The choice of aesthetic came from researching about the time of year people drink red wine most during. My research showed that people drink red wine most during the winter, so I decided to go with warm lighting and a winter mountaion environment.

Camera Movement in Film

Understanding how cameras move in film so that I can replicate it.

Notes I took from the video:

  • Static, Zero Movement
    • Precise compositions
    • Dialog
  • Pan - Fixed Rotation
    • Wes Anderson
    • Reveals
    • Follow movement
  • Whip pan - fast moving
    • Damien Chazelle
    • High emotion, relation between subjects
  • Tilt - vertical, fixed
    • Good for Reveals
    • Emotion - dominance of characters
    • Reveal, setting, scale
  • push in - towards fixed object
    • Cue importance
    • Provoke tension or thoughts
  • pull out - away from fixed object
    • De-emphasize the subject
    • Reveal context - setting
  • zoom - change focal length
    • Reveal content
    • Camera movement unnatural
    • Focus on a detail
    • Stanley Kubrick
  • crash zoom - fast zoom
    • Dramatic effect
    • Fast subject movement
  • dolly zoom - camera + zoom
    • Vertigo effect
    • Zoom out & dolly in
    • Common for conflict scenes
  • zoom in & dolly out
    • Subject dominant
    • Create relation
    • Focus on subject
  • Camera roll - long axis rotation
    • Diss-orientating
  • Tracking - Move with subject
    • Feeling of "Where is the character going?" and "What will happen when they get there?"
    • Immersive into scene
    • Anticipation
  • Trucking - camera moving, left or right
    • Show setting
  • Arc - camera orbit around object
    • Focus centered on subject
    • Adding speed makes a dizzing effect
  • Boom - vertical camera movement
    • Reveal info
    • Can follow subject
    • Can show attempted movement of subject
  • Random movement
    • Intimate effect
    • Documentary feel
    • Unplanned feel
    • Anything can happen

Compositions

Learning how film directors use compositions in film.

Notes I took from the video:

  • Focus points
    • Points of interest
    • Effective in wide shots
    • Minimal shots
  • Rule of thirds
    • Intersections great to put focal points on
  • Golden triangle
    • Two balanced focal points
  • leading lines
    • Natural lines leading towards subject
    • Roads, bridge, buildings
  • negative space
    • Lots of negative space can make a lonely feeling
    • Lots of positive space can provoke a emotional or important feeling
  • Framing
    • Framing within the frame
    • Draws attention
  • depth
    • Foreground, midground, and background
    • Bluring background
    • Small foreground
    • Changing focus
  • Colour & contrast
    • Silhouettes
    • light background, dark subject
    • Complimentary colour in lighting to seperate subject from background

Best season to drink red wine?

[Click to View]

Red wine is often prefered during the winter time as it compliments the heavier type of food usually eaten. The wine will also often be a full-bodied red, with tannins and higher alcohol levels.

I wanted to when the best time was to drink red wine so that I could design the animation around that season.

Inside Warmer Lighting

This is the change to the lighting that I made after researching and realising that red wine is had more in the winter. The idea here is warmer lighting that is reminiscent of light from a fire.

Resources used:

  • Model: Wine Barrel 01
  • Textures: Wooden Gate
  • Textures: Wooden Planks

Lighting & Models

As I was designing this scene I knew that I wanted to make the fire place a key element. This is because red wine is typically had during winter, and that's when people use their fires the most.

This influenced the colour of the lighting. Deciding to go with a warmer orange light, than a standard white or blue lighting (like in the first image).
